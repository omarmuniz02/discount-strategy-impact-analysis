# SQL Project Work Frame

## Scenario #1

**Stakeholder (VP of Sales)**

> “Our sales have been growing, but profitability doesn’t seem to be improving the way we expected. We’ve been offering more discounts to drive growth, and I’m starting to wonder whether those discounts are actually helping us or just eroding our margins. I’d like to understand what’s going on and whether we need to rethink our pricing strategy.”

---

## Clarifying Questions

I asked clarifying questions to better understand:

- What does “profitability” mean to this stakeholder—gross profit, margin %, or something else?
- Are they concerned about specific product categories, channels, or customer segments?
- What time period should be compared?
- What level of discounting is considered aggressive?
- What decisions are they trying to make—reduce discounts, change pricing, target certain customers?
- Do they already suspect certain channels or categories are driving the issue?

---

## Refined Problem Statement

Analyze whether discounts above 20% in the Online channel—particularly within Electronics—generate enough additional sales volume and customer activity to offset declines in gross profit and margin percentage over the 2023–2024 period.

---

## Analytical Thinking to Test

1. High discounts increase revenue.
2. High discounts compress margins.
3. Electronics is especially sensitive to discounting.
4. Online sales rely more heavily on discounts.
5. Returning customers may respond differently than new customers.

---

## Project Objective

Determine whether discounts above 20% in the Online channel—particularly within the Electronics category—generate enough additional revenue and sales volume to justify the resulting decline in gross profit and margin percentage.

---

# Insights Log

## Revenue

**Observation**
- The business generated approximately $2.85 billion in revenue during 2023–2024.

**Possible Driver**
- Revenue is influenced by transaction volume, unit quantities, pricing, and discount strategy.

**Business Impact**
- The business operates at a scale where small percentage improvements can translate into tens of millions of dollars.

---

## COGS

**Observation**
- Total COGS is approximately $2.58 billion.

**Possible Driver**
- Large sales volume and substantial per-unit costs, especially in higher-priced categories.

**Business Impact**
- More than 90% of revenue is consumed by inventory cost, indicating relatively narrow gross margins (2.58 / 2.85 ≈ 90.5%).

---

## Gross Profit

**Observation**
- The business generated approximately $271.7 million in gross profit during 2023–2024.

**Possible Driver**
- Large sales volume offset by high product costs and discounting.

**Business Impact**
- Gross profit represents only a small share of revenue, suggesting profitability is highly sensitive to pricing and cost changes.

---

## Margin %

**Observation**
- Overall gross margin is 9.53%.

**Possible Driver**
- High product costs and discounting absorb most revenue.

**Business Impact**
- The company retains only about 9.5 cents of gross profit per dollar of sales, making profitability highly sensitive to pricing and discount decisions.

---

## Units Sold

**Observation**
- The business sold approximately 10.19 million units during 2023–2024.
- $2.851 billion / 10.193 million = approximately $279.8 revenue per unit sold.

**Possible Driver**
- High transaction frequency and sizable quantities per order.

**Business Impact**
- Revenue growth may be driven more by product volume than by price changes alone.

---

## Average Discount

**Observation**
- The average discount across all transactions is 15.0%.

**Possible Driver**
- The company regularly uses promotions to stimulate sales and customer purchasing behavior.

**Business Impact**
- Discounting is a substantial component of the pricing strategy and likely has a significant influence on margin performance.

---

## Grouped by Sales Channel

**Observation**
- Retail and Online have virtually identical gross margins (~9.53%).

**Possible Driver**
- Channel-level performance is broadly balanced; profitability issues are likely concentrated within smaller segments rather than the channel overall.

**Business Impact**
- The Online channel does not appear structurally less profitable than Retail when viewed in aggregate.

---

## Grouped by Sales Channel and Product Category

**Observation**
- Online Electronics has nearly identical revenue, discount levels, and margin percentage compared with Retail Electronics.

**Conclusion**
- There is no evidence that Online Electronics is disproportionately reliant on discounting.

**Business Impact**
- Channel-level pricing and profitability appear highly consistent.

**Recommendation**
- Shift focus toward discount thresholds, customer segments, or time trends to uncover more nuanced behavior.

---

## Grouped by Discount Tier (High / Medium / Low)

**Discount Tiers**
- High Discount: >20%
- Medium Discount: 10%–20%
- Low Discount: <10%

**Observation**
- Transactions discounted above 20% produce negative margins across all categories and channels.

**Business Impact**
- The company loses money on these sales despite higher unit volumes.

**Recommendation**
- Restrict discounts above 20% and prioritize medium discount levels to balance growth and profitability.

---

# Personal Takeaway

I did not force the data to match an assumption.

I investigated the stakeholder’s concern, tested it methodically, and allowed the evidence to guide the conclusion.
